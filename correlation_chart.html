Each bar shows how closely a country's stock market moves in relation to its unemployment rate. Bars pointing left (blue) mean stocks generally fall when unemployment rises (Negative Correlation). Bars pointing right (red) mean they tend to move in the same direction (Positive Correlation). The min overlapping years lets you filter the countries that show up based on the amount of years that there is recorded data of that country.
